## Payment Source Eligibility

### ✅ COMMISSION ELIGIBLE (Private Pay)

| Payment Source | Notes |
|----------------|-------|
| **Private Pay (Cash/Savings)** | ✅ Full commission |
| **VA Aid & Attendance** | ✅ Up to $2,874/mo supplement |
| **Veteran's Pension** | ✅ Considered private pay |
| **Long-Term Care Insurance** | ✅ Full commission |
| **Social Security/Pension** | ✅ Considered private pay |
| **Selling Home for Care** | ✅ Full commission |
| **Family Funds** | ✅ Full commission |

### ❌ NO COMMISSION (Florida Law)

| Payment Source | Why No Commission |
|----------------|-------------------|
| **Medicaid Waiver** | FL Statute 429.195 prohibits |
| **Medicaid Diversion** | Illegal per patient brokering law |
| **Medicaid Pending** | Cannot collect until clear (some places accept Medicaid pending depending on private pay amount) |

> **Legal Reference**: FL Statute 429.195 explicitly allows referral fees ONLY when "the referred consumers are NOT Medicaid recipients." Medicaid Pending status is acceptable for referral fees, provided the commission is calculated strictly from private pay sources and NOT based on any Medicaid reimbursement amount. Violating this is patient brokering under FL Statute 817.505 (felony).

## AHCA Form 1823

### What It Is

The **AHCA Form 1823** is a mandatory physician health assessment.

### Key Points

- Doctor completes it (NOT you)
- Required within **60 days BEFORE** or **30 days AFTER** admission
- **Best Practice**: Obtain it *before* touring facilities so you can confirm the facility's ability to meet the senior's specific care needs
- Updated annually
- **Your role**: Educate families about the requirement

### What It Covers

1. Physical/mental status
2. ADL assistance needs
3. Medications
4. Cognitive/behavioral status
5. Special care requirements

## Your Intake Form (29 Questions)

### Section 1: Family Contact

- Full name
- Relationship to senior
- Phone/email
- Best contact time
- How they found you

### Section 2: Senior Info

- Senior's name, DOB, gender
- Current living situation
- Current location

### Section 3: Care Needs

- Primary reason for placement
- Care level needed
- ADL assistance required
- Mobility status
- Memory/cognitive status
- Medical conditions
- Special care needs

### Section 4: Budget & Payment ⚠️ CRITICAL

**Ask specifically:**

```
"How will care be paid for?"
[ ] Private savings/family funds ✅
[ ] VA Aid & Attendance ✅
[ ] Long-term care insurance ✅
[ ] Social Security + Pension ✅
[ ] Selling home ✅
[ ] Medicaid Waiver ❌ (no commission)
[ ] Medicaid pending ❌ (wait until resolved)
[ ] proof of income(bank state ments or social security award letter, pension letter ect. will be needed prior to move in for easier placement
```

### Section 5: Location

- Preferred city/area
- Max distance from family
- Timeline (immediate, soon, planning)

### Section 6: Preferences

- Room type
- Amenities wanted
- Additional notes

## Referral Workflow

```
1. Family Inquiry → 2. Intake Call (15 min)
       ↓
3. Verify Payment Source
   ✅ Private/VA/LTC → Continue
   ❌ Medicaid → Help (but note no commission on medicaid portion.)
       ↓
4. Match 3-5 Facilities → 5. Present Options
       ↓
6. Schedule Tours → 7. Assist with Decision
       ↓
8. Move-In Coordination → 9. Remind about 1823 Form
       ↓
10. Invoice Facility (on move-in)
       ↓
11. Collect Commission (usually Net 30)
```

## Key Talking Points

### When Asked About Payment
>
> "Do you know how Mom's care will be funded? Many families use savings, sell the home, or use long-term care insurance, or a family member to contribute family contribution. Is she a veteran or married to one? VA Aid & Attendance can add up to $2,800 a month to help cover costs."

### About Your Service
>
> "My service is completely free to you. I get paid by the facility when your loved one moves in - similar to how a real estate agent works."

### About Form 1823
>
> "The facility will give you a 1823 form. It's a health assessment that the doctor fills out. Simple - just take it to the doctor within 60 days before move-in."
